Section 02

Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide Directly

When you complete the contact form on this website, I collect the following information:

  • Full Name — to identify you and personalise our communication.
  • Email Address — to respond to your enquiry.
  • Phone Number — to contact you for follow-up on your enquiry if required.
  • Company Name (optional) — to understand the context of your enquiry.
  • Service of Interest — to route your enquiry appropriately.
  • Message Content — the details of your enquiry or project brief.

When you visit this website, standard technical information may be automatically recorded by the hosting infrastructure and third-party services, including:

  • IP address and approximate geographic location

This website uses Formspree to process contact form submissions. Formspree operates as a data processor and may collect form data in accordance with its own privacy policy. Contact form data is transmitted to and stored temporarily by Formspree before being forwarded to my email address.

Section 03

How We Use Your Information

Personal information collected through this website is used exclusively for the following purposes:

  • Responding to enquiries: To review and respond to messages submitted via the contact form.
  • Business communication: To follow up regarding potential freelance, consulting, or employment opportunities.
  • Service delivery: If a professional engagement is agreed upon, to deliver agreed marketing or development services.

  • Website improvement: Anonymised and aggregated data may be used to understand how the website is used and to improve its content and functionality.

Important: I do not use your personal data for automated decision-making, profiling, or any form of targeted advertising without your explicit consent. I do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to any third party.

Section 04

Legal Basis for Processing

The lawful bases under which personal data is processed depend on your jurisdiction:

  • Consent (GDPR Art. 6(1)(a)): You have given clear consent by submitting the contact form and agreeing to this policy.
  • Legitimate Interests (GDPR Art. 6(1)(f)): Processing is necessary for the legitimate interests of responding to business enquiries.
  • Contractual Necessity (GDPR Art. 6(1)(b)): Where a service agreement is entered into, processing is necessary to perform that contract.
  • Legal Obligation: Processing may be required to comply with applicable law in India, the UK, the UAE, or the USA.

Under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (India), processing is based on your freely given, informed, specific, and unambiguous consent provided when submitting the contact form.

Section 05

Data Sharing & Third Parties

I do not sell, rent, or share your personal data with third parties for marketing purposes. Limited data sharing may occur only in the following circumstances:

  • Formspree Inc.: Contact form submissions are processed via Formspree, acting as a data processor. Formspree is a US-based service that complies with GDPR and maintains appropriate safeguards including Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) for international transfers.
  • Email Service Provider: Enquiries are delivered to a Gmail account operated by Google LLC. Google processes email data in accordance with its own privacy policy and complies with applicable data protection laws.
  • Legal requirements: I may disclose personal data if required by law, court order, or government authority in any applicable jurisdiction.
  • Business transfer: If this business is transferred or acquired, personal data held may form part of the transferred assets, subject to the same privacy protections.
Section 06

Data Retention

Personal data is retained only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purpose for which it was collected, or as required by applicable law:

  • Enquiries with no subsequent engagement: Data retained for a maximum of 12 months from the date of submission, after which it is securely deleted.
  • Enquiries leading to a professional engagement: Data related to a contractual engagement is retained for a period of 7 years in accordance with applicable tax and accounting obligations (as required under Indian taxation law, HMRC requirements for UK, and IRS guidelines for the USA).
  • Website analytics data: Anonymised usage data is not subject to a fixed retention limit as it cannot be used to identify individuals.

You may request deletion of your data at any time by contacting me at the address below, subject to any overriding legal retention obligations.

Section 07

Your Rights & Choices

Depending on your location, you have various rights regarding your personal data. Regardless of jurisdiction, I am committed to honouring all reasonable requests:

  • Right of Access: You may request a copy of the personal data I hold about you.
  • Right to Rectification: You may request correction of any inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten"): You may request deletion of your personal data, subject to legal retention obligations.
  • Right to Restriction of Processing: You may request that processing of your data be restricted in certain circumstances.
  • Right to Data Portability: You may request a copy of your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to Object: You may object to processing based on legitimate interests.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.
  • Right against Automated Decision-Making: No automated profiling or decision-making affecting you is carried out through this website.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact: ishwar.mule007@gmail.com. I will respond within 30 days (or the timeframe mandated by applicable law in your jurisdiction).

In addition to the general rights above, the following jurisdiction-specific rights and regulatory frameworks apply:

Under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, you have the right to access your data, correct inaccuracies, nominate a representative, and seek grievance redressal. UK & EU — UK GDPR / GDPR

Under UK GDPR and EU GDPR, you have rights of access, rectification, erasure, portability, restriction, and objection. USA — CCPA / State Laws

California residents have rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) / CPRA, including the right to know what personal information is collected, to delete it, and to opt out of sale (no sale occurs). Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut, and other state residents have similar rights under applicable state privacy laws.

🇦🇪
UAE — PDPL / DIFC

Under UAE Federal Decree-Law No. 45 of 2021 (Personal Data Protection Law) and the DIFC Data Protection Law 2020, you have rights of access, correction, and erasure. Data processing is conducted on the basis of legitimate interests and consent. Cross-border transfers comply with UAE data transfer requirements.

For all other jurisdictions, I apply the higher standard of protection offered by GDPR as a baseline, in addition to any specific local legal requirements.
